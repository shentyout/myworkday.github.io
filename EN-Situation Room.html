Plastic Injection VS Production situation

In recent years, due to factors such as the trade war between the United States and China and the COVID-19 pandemic, Taiwan has also benefited from some favorable conditions. We have collaborated with a plastic injection factory to customize a real-time information system based on their production line requirements.

We integrate the necessary control information and divide it into four categories, which are displayed in the production management department's situation room and on-site. This allows production and quality control inspectors and on-site supervisors to understand the status of each machine in the production line at any time.

User interview

We conducted discussions and analysis on the injection factory's management, production management department, and injection engineers.

Factory's management
From a business perspective, it is essential to understand if each machine's production capacity, idle power consumption, operating time, and output are proportional. This is because there may be issues such as overproduction, excessive material consumption, rework, and long idle time that can negatively impact profits. Personnel and machines can't be immediately scheduled to other production lines, resulting in idle machines that can't produce.

Production management
The interviewees are two production managements, discussing the situation that occurred in the production line. As SMEs have limited manpower, employees need to handle cross-departmental work. They can't keep track of the production status and output of each production line effectively. This leads to delays in order schedules and jams, as well as difficulties in understanding the status of each machine, which in turn affects scheduling.

Injection engineers
Each machine has an injection engineer and an operator. Engineer will always check the parameters of each machine, and operator is responsible for the initial processing and simple measurement of the finished product. However, the condition of the machine is constantly changing, and the engineer can't immediately monitor the situation of the machine under their responsibility. Unable to help each other immediately, causing wasted time.

Expected production vs Actual production

Sometimes unexpected situations may arise during the expected production process, such as need to produce other orders on short notice. These situations can result in delayed delivery, therefore, we discussed how to confirm if the orders produced by the machine in line with the expected schedule.
Machine breakdowns, last-minute orders, and material or menpower shortages are some of the factors that can cause order delays.Although we cannot immediately determine which factor caused the current situation, we can at least let the managers understand the gap between the plan and the actual operation.

Transparent and real-time production line

In the past, without real-time visualization, it was difficult to accurately calculate machine capacity, electricity costs, and labor costs.
After we built a visualization, information became more transparent and immediate. For example, if a machine has a problem that can't be solved immediately, the workers on the production line can quickly be moved to another machine to reduce idle time. We can also quickly identify which machine has a problem and calculate the daily operating status. We can use the production order to determine whether to control inventory in real-time and when to inspect and control materials.

Currently, we are working with the machine vendors to improve the accuracy of signal feedback. In the future, we aim to achieve full automation in monitoring machine status, eliminating human factors and making information more objective and precise.
