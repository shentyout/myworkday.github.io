Traditional industries go digital.

We're improving production based on what customers need most, Management feedback: Issues include machine idle time, unclear production line status, material waste, lack of material data system, and uneven manpower allocation caused by unclear machine conditions.

As there are various ERP systems available in the market for different industries to use, the system has good features but lacks flexibility. Additionally, due to a shortage of staff, one department needs to handle the business of two to three departments, making it difficult to distinguish and manage their scope of work, moreover, the current ERP interface is cluttered, and novice users are often overwhelmed by its complex structure and complicated operation process. We are improving the production process and making the machines on the production line more visible to solve the problems of complicated operations and unclear machine status.

Our partners

Based on the opinions of the production management department and the on-site injection engineers, the requirements and information are categorized into machine information, order information, production status, and machine monitoring time.

Basic system services and scope framework

We will connect the machine operation status to our system, allowing managers to have real-time access to the current status of all machines in the factory. We are optimizing and designing the production management process. Firstly, we are creating four main data categories: 1. customer information, 2. supplier information, 3. product information, 4. material consumption information. These four types of data form the foundation of the system, and all operational processes will retrieve information from them.
In terms of the process, we will start with the "Order Creation" phase, which includes Order Creation → Inventory Inquiry → Export of Production Orders. These are the parts of the project that we will work on.。

User Interviews

We target plastic injection factories who have the capability to develop molds and produce products. We aim to optimize their production and machine operations for real-time information.

Our interviewees consisted of the second-generation owner of the injection factory, two production managements, one material managements, one production line inspections, and two injection engineers, a total of seven people. The current ERP system is very complete, but not all its features are needed by the company. After analyzing the system, we discovered that users only use a few specific functions. Some unnecessary features were purchased at the time, not by choice. We are currently optimizing the system for the production process and integrating it with the machine operation to make production line management smoother and more transparent.

We are currently optimizing the operation of the production, customer, material management systems. And will also develop other system features in the future.

Key points organization and scope limitations

User flow

Currently, we establish customer data, vendor data, product data, and material data. In the future, the order process and production process will be linked and extracted from the above data, ensuring consistency throughout the entire production process.

Creation of four main databases

Vendor data, customer data, product data, and raw material data are the four essential foundations of the entire system process. For instance, in the order data, we need to record which customer and which products are involved. In the product data, we must note the required raw materials. The raw material consumables data includes vendor contact information and transaction records.
Before commencing the production process, it is crucial to establish the above data to be used for future production management.

Creating customer and vendor data

Allowing future production processes to retrieve customer and vendor information. Users can input the customer number or name to easily fetch all existing information from this database, reducing input time. Sometimes, vendors may also be customers, so we have provided an option for customers who are also vendors. Users do not need to create duplicate records in both databases.

1.Vendor Classification
-Vendors are sorted into types such as hardware, plastic materials.
-To find vendors, select a category and search by number or name.
-Category-based search is more intuitive than traditional ERP searches.

2.Customer Information
-Customer data is similar to vendor data.
-The only difference is that vendors need to be categorized.

3.Customer and Vendor Combined
-Create one record to be shared between both databases, avoiding duplicate inputs.

Creation of material and consumables data

1.Creation of Raw Material and Consumables Data
-All materials require detailed records.
-Information includes supplier, material type, vendor details.
-The lower section categorizes warehouses for each raw material.
-Material units are defined, ex: grams, pieces, for future calculations.

2.Raw Material and Consumables Categories
-Plastic materials
-Color powders, pigments
-General hardware
-Mold hardware
-Plastic packaging, paper packaging
-Unclassified items are grouped under "Others."

3.Vendor Information
-Existing vendors can be searched in the vendor database.
-Importing data automatically retrieves detailed information about the vendor.

4.Searching for Materials:
-First, select the material category.
-Then, choose to search by material number or name.

Order process

Summary: Contains production data for multiple products in one order.
Individual: Production progress varies for each product. It helps track specific order progress accurately.

1.Data creation
-Enter customer number or key name to fetch data.
-Create all product details and quantities ordered by the customer.
-Set the delivery date for each product.

2.Import products
-Import products from the database.
-Complete product data before creating orders.
-Multiple products can be imported at once.
-Production details fetched from the product database.

3.Export documents
-Export manufacturing, purchase, and sales orders after order creation.
-Manufacturing orders are used for on-site production.
-Purchase orders facilitate material procurement.
-Sales orders ensure sufficient inventory for shipment.

4.Inventory search
-Production can check product inventory.
-Separate queries for product and material inventory.
-Users confirm inventory during export.

5.Exported documents can be split
-Exported orders can be split.
-Orders can be split for different production lines based on their status.
-Provides flexibility in controlling production lines.

Manufacturing orders are vital for production operations. The production line follows these orders to carry out manufacturing tasks. There are two main types of manufacturing orders: those exported from orders and those manually created.
